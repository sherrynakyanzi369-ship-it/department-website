Our History

The Department of Computing & Informatics began as a small technology training unit, dedicated to equipping students with foundational skills in East Africa. It later evolved into an independent faculty wing, offering comprehensive degrees in Computer Science and IT.

After rapid technological shifts in the 2000s, the program continuously updated its curriculum to include Software Engineering, Cyber Security, and AI. The department later relocated to its state-of-the-art laboratory complex, expanding its hardware and research facilities. Today, it stands as an elite division producing industry leaders and receiving top academic accreditations.

The Department of Computing & Informatics nurtures the head, heart, and hand—developing profound knowledge, ethical character, and practical technical skills for service and leadership in the digital age.
